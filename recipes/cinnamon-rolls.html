Cinnamon Rolls

Image by me ^u^

Ingredients

For the dough

  • 2 teaspoons of yeast (7 grams)
  • 1 cup of warm milk (250 mL)
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (6 grams)
  • 1/2 cup of white sugar (100 grams)
  • 1/3 cup of unsalted butter (75 grams)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt (6 grams)
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour

For the filling

  • 1 cup of brown sugar (105 grams)
  • 2 teaspoons of cinnamon powder (10 grams)
  • 1/3 cup of unsalted butter (75 grams)

For the icing

  • 6 tablespoons of unsalted butter (85 grams)
  • 1 and 1/2 cup of powdered sugar (165 grams)
  • 1/4 cup of cream cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Heat the milk until it's warm (105-115 ℉).
  2. Add the teaspoon of sugar and the yeast, mix well, cover the bowl with a cloth and let it rest for 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl mix the flour, sugar cup, and salt.
  4. In another bowl mix the butter (make sure it's room temperature) and the eggs.
  5. Add this mixture to the large bowl and mix, then add the yeast milk. (Make sure the milk has risen, otherwise it means you didn't heat the milk correctly or the yeast has gone bad, discard and start again if that's the case).
  6. Mix until the ingredients have integrated together, then knead for around 10 minutes and the dough is elastic.
  7. In an oiled bowl let the dough rest for 1 hour or until it doubles its size.
  8. Mix the brown sugar, and the cinnamon powder.
  9. When the dough has doubled its size, knead for 3 minutes to deflate it.
  10. In a flat surface, spread some flour and stretch in a rectangular shape until it's 5 milimeters thick.
  11. Spread the butter over the dough surface and spread the brown sugar and cinnamon mixture until it's evenly distributed.
  12. Roll up lenghtwise, and cut the rolls to you prefered size (usually 1 and a 1/2 inches).
  13. Let the rolls rest on an oiled tray for 40 minutes.
  14. Preheat the oven to 350 ℉ and bake the rolls for around 20 minutes or until the borders are brown.
  15. For the icing, mix the butter and the cream cheese until its uniform, then add the vanilla extract, powdered sugar, and salt, keep mixing until it's uniform.
  16. Add the icing after the rolls have cooled down a bit. (5 to 7 minutes).
  17. Enjoy!
